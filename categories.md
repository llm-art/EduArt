This repository adopts a three-complementary-axis annotation framework to describe and analyze art-history questions for QVA benchmark.

Each question is annotated along three independent and orthogonal axes, capturing what the question is about, where the knowledge belongs, and how the knowledge must be cognitively deployed.

## AXIS 1 — Art-Historical Ontology

**What the question is about**

This axis captures the core art-historical properties traditionally used to classify artworks.
Where applicable, categories are aligned with ICONCLASS and the Getty Art & Architecture Thesaurus (AAT).

### 1. Subject matter and Iconography

#### Definition
The depicted subject matter of the artwork, including events, figures, narratives, or symbolic motifs.

#### Example
This work represents:
* Nativity
* Lamentation
* Crucifixion
* Annunciation

### 2. Object & Work Type

#### Definition
The type of artistic object, independent of subject or style.

#### Example
The work on the right is a:
* diptych
* triptych
* predella
* polyptych

### 3. Materials & Techniques

#### Definition
The materials employed and the technical processes used to produce the work.

#### Example
The primary medium of the work is
* watercolor
* encaustic
* tempera
* oil

### 4. Style & Period

#### Definition
The art-historical style, movement, or chronological period with which the work is associated.

#### Example
The painting is most closely associated with which art-historical period?
* Baroque
* Realism
* Romanticism
* Impressionism

### 5. Authorship

#### Definition
Identification of the creator of a work as a named artist or authorial entity.

#### Example
The painting is by
* Géricault
* Daumier
* Delacroix
* Manet

## AXIS 2 — Cultural & Disciplinary Context

**Where the knowledge belongs**

This axis captures the cultural tradition and academic field presupposed by the question.

### 6. Cultural Tradition

#### Definition
The broad cultural-historical tradition in which the artwork and question are situated.

#### Possible values
* Western
* Eastern 
* Middle-East

#### Example
Identify the art-historical period of a Romanesque church.

### 7. Disciplinary Domain

#### Definition
The academic field whose knowledge is primarily required to answer the question.

#### Possible values
	•	Art History
	•	Architectural History

#### Example
The plan of this church is best described as
A. Greek cross
B. basilican
C. centralized
D. octagonal

## AXIS 3 — Epistemic Level

**The level of cognitive effort required**

Can have one of the following values:

### 3.1 Factual Identification

#### Definition
Questions requiring retrieval or recognition of a single, discrete, object-bound fact.

#### Epistemic profile
* Atomic knowledge
* Low epistemic dependence
* No generalization required

#### Example
The subject of the painting is
A. the Annunciation
B. the Nativity
C. the Crucifixion
D. the Resurrection

### 3.2 Contextual Knowledge

#### Definition
Questions requiring relational understanding based on historical norms, conventions, or classification across multiple cases.

#### Epistemic profile
* Relational knowledge
* Requires generalization
* Grounded in accepted scholarship

#### Example
Church interiors from this period typically were covered with
A. timber roofs
B. quadripartite vaults
C. domes
D. barrel vaults

### 3.3 Interpretive Reasoning

#### Definition
Questions requiring inference or evaluation of meaning, expressive intent, ideology, or theoretical position.

#### Epistemic profile
	•	Hermeneutic reasoning
	•	Meaning is inferred, not retrieved

#### Example
The golden background of the painting conveys a sense of
A. spatial recession
B. mystical vision
C. observed naturalism
D. site specificity
